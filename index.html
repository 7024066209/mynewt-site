Apache Mynewt is a community-driven, permissively licensed open source initiative for constrained, embedded applications. The emergence of the Internet of Things is proving that anything that can be connected will be connected . Many of these connected devices—wristbands and other wearables, light bulbs and locks—must be operated for long periods of time, but are constrained in terms of power, memory, and storage. Apache Mynewt flexibly addresses these constraints while remaining hardware agnostic.

Apache Mynewt consists of four primary collaborative goals:

Produce a foundational RTOS and embedded middleware utilities such as boot loaders, file systems, and time-series data support

Build solid networking stack support for secure, efficient communications with constrained devices

Provide simple image and configuration management and deep instrumentation for ongoing diagnostics, whether at the workbench or after mass deployment

Ensure modularity and easy composability to build an optimized image aligned with the developer’s application requirements and hardware selection
